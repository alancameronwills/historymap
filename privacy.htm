Privacy Policy

User privacy

We do not send any data about you or your use of our site to any third party. The exception is that we use an analytics service, Azure Application Insights, to count the number of our users. Neither we nor Azure store or pass on this information to anyone else.

We will not send emails to you, unless we wish to discuss with you information that you have contributed to the map.

Cookies

We use cookies on our website. A cookie is a short text file sent by a web server to a web browser and stored by the browser. The text file is then sent back to the server each time the browser requests a page from the server. This enables the web server to identify and track the web browser.

Cookies are used to help us count the number of users and the numbers of places they look at.

Cookies are used to store the state of your session, so that when you use the map again, it will begin in the position that you previously left it.

If you sign in to the map, your identity will be kept in a cookie so that when you open the map again, you will be automatically signed in.

Contributions

If you wish to contribute to the map, you must:

  • Not include information about current or recent living residents of the places on the map. You must avoid identifying current or living recent residents by their names or contact details. Information on the map should relate to 100 years or more ago.
  • Not include material that might offend anyone for any reason.
  • Ensure that you have the consent of the copyright holder of any photo or document that you contribute. Note that the copyright of a private photo is generally with the photographer or their heir, who is often not the current holder of a print or negative. Postcard and newspaper publishers usually hold the copyright of photos originally printed by them. The holder of a copy that you may ask you to include acknowledgement in the accompanying text.

If you contribute to the map, you agree that:

  • the text and media you supply will be published on the Web
  • People who read your contribution may re-use it according to the terms of the Creative Commons Attribution + ShareAlike licence

Amendments
